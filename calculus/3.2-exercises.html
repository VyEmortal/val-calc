3.2 Exercises — Indeterminate Forms and L'Hôpital's Rule

Click on a problem number to view the solution.

For each of exercises 1–15, evaluate the limit or state that it does not exist.

\(1.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2x}{\sin(x)}\)
\(2.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x - 2}{3\sin(x - 2)}\)
\(3.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\sqrt[3]{x + 3}}{\ln(x)}\)
\(4.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{x - 4}{8x - 16 - x^2} \)
\(5.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to \pi/2} \tan(x) \left(x - \frac{\pi}{2}\right) \)
\(6.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} x^2e^{-x} \)
\(7.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} x^3 \csc(x) \)
\(8.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to -\infty} x^2e^{3x} \)
\(9.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 2^-} \sin(x - 2) \ln(2 - x) \)
\(10.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 1^+} \left(x - 1\right)^{3/(x^2 - 1)} \)
\(11.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 2} \left(1 + \sin(x - 2)\right)^{1/(x^2 - 4)} \)
\(12.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 0^+} \left(\sin 2x \right)^{1/x}\)
\(13.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 1^+} \left(\ln x \right)^{1/(x - 1)} \)
\(14.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\sqrt{x}\right)^{e^{-x}} \)
\(15.\)
\(\ds \lim_{x \to 0^+} \left(\csc x \right)^{x}\)
\(16.\)
It is known that \(g(4) = 2\) and \(g'(4) = 6.\) Find \(\ds \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{2 - g(x)}{\sin(x - 4)}\) or state that it does not exist.
\(17.\)
Functions \(f\) and \(g\) are differentiable functions. It is known that \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 0,\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} g(x) = 0,\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} f'(x) = 5,\) and \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} g'(x) = 8.\) Find \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right)^2 \) or state that it does not exist.
\(18.\)
It is known that \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f(x) = 3,\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f'(x) = 0,\) and \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f''(x) = 1.\) Find \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4f(x) - 12 \cos(x)}{x^2}\) or state that it does not exist.
\(19.\)
Let \(p\) and \(q\) be differentiable functions. Let \(q\) be a function defined by \(\ds q(x) = \frac{2p(x)^3 + 4}{x^2 - 6x}\) that satisfies \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} q(x) = 12.\) It is known that \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} q(x)\) can be evaluated using L'Hôpital's Rule. Find \(p(0)\) and \(p'(0).\)
\(20.\)
Determine whether \(f(x) = 2 \sqrt[3]{x}\) or \(g(x) = \ln(x - 4)\) grows faster.
\(21.\)
Determine whether \(f(x) = 2x^6 - 4x^5 + 2\) or \(g(x) = e^x\) grows faster.
\(22.\)
Show that \(x^n\) grows faster than \(\ln(x)\) for all positive \(n.\)
\(23.\)
Show that \(e^x\) grows faster than \(x^n\) for all positive, finite \(n.\)
