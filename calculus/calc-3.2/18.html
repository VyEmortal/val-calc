Problem 18 — 3.2 Exercises (Indeterminate Forms and L'Hôpital's Rule)

It is known that \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f(x) = 3,\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f'(x) = 0,\) and \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f''(x) = 1.\) Find \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4f(x) - 12 \cos(x)}{x^2}\) or state that it does not exist.

SOLUTION The limit is in the indeterminate form \(0/0.\) Therefore, applying L'Hôpital's Rule, we get \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4f(x) - 12 \cos(x)}{x^2} &= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{\dd}{\dd x} (4f(x) - 12 \cos(x))}{\frac{\dd}{\dd x} x^2} \\ &= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4 f'(x) + 12 \sin(x)}{2x}, \ea \] which is in the indeterminate form \(0/0.\) Thus, applying L'Hôpital's Rule again yields \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4 f'(x) + 12 \sin(x)}{2x} &= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{\dd}{\dd x}\left(4 f'(x) + 12 \sin(x) \right)}{\frac{\dd}{\dd x} 2x} \\ &= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4 f''(x) + 12 \cos(x)}{2} \\ &= \frac{4(1) + 12(1)}{2} \\ &= \box{8.} \ea \]
