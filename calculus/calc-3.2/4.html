Problem 4 — 3.2 Exercises (Indeterminate Forms and L'Hôpital's Rule)

Evaluate \(\ds \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{x - 4}{8x - 16 - x^2} \) or state that it does not exist.

SOLUTION Because the limit is in the indeterminate form \(0/0,\) we can apply L'Hôpital's Rule by differentiating the numerator and denominator to get \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{x - 4}{8x - 16 - x^2} &= \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\frac{\dd}{\dd x}(x - 4)}{\frac{\dd}{\dd x}(8x - 16 - x^2)} \\ &= \underbrace{\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{1}{8 - 2x}}_{1/0} \\ \ea \] Because the resulting limit, \(\ds \lim_{x \to 4} 1/(8 - 2x),\) is in the form \(1/0,\) the limit does not exist.
