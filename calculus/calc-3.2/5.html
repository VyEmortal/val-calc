Problem 5 — 3.2 Exercises (Indeterminate Forms and L'Hôpital's Rule)

Evaluate \(\ds \lim_{x \to \pi/2} \tan(x) \left(x - \frac{\pi}{2}\right) \) or state that it does not exist.

SOLUTION We can rewrite the limit as \[\lim_{x \to \pi/2} \frac{\sin(x) \left(x - \frac{\pi}{2}\right)}{\cos(x)},\] which is in the indeterminate form \(0/0.\) Therefore, we can apply L'Hôpital's Rule to obtain \[ \lim_{x \to \pi/2} \frac{\cos(x) \left(x - \frac{\pi}{2}\right) + \sin(x)}{-\sin(x)} = \box{-1.} \]
